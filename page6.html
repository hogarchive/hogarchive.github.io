What was the backstory?

art drawn by hogfan224

"The Hog" was the brainchild of Wyatt J. Wiliker Davids, a visionary animator with an insatiable appetite for storytelling and a knack for bringing characters to life. The seed of inspiration for the show sprouted from Davids' childhood memories of his Mexican heritage and the folklore and legends that permeated his upbringing. Drawing upon these cultural influences, he sought to create a character that embodied the vibrancy, humor, and resilience of his Mexican roots.

With a bold stroke of creativity, Davids envisioned a big, green protagonist who possessed a distinct roundness reminiscent of a bowling ball. This unique physicality allowed The Hog to roll and bounce his way through adventures, injecting a playful and dynamic element into the show's narrative. Davids intended The Hog's rotund figure to serve as a visual metaphor for embracing one's uniqueness, highlighting the importance of self-acceptance and celebrating individuality.

To further develop The Hog's persona, Davids drew inspiration from his own family history, particularly his grandfather's military background. Combining this influence with a touch of whimsy, he adorned The Hog with an army hat and glasses, bestowing upon him an air of authority and wisdom, juxtaposed against his jovial and carefree nature. This charming dichotomy captured the essence of The Hog's character, making him relatable to audiences young and old.

In shaping the world of "The Hog," Davids envisioned a fantastical realm where imagination and reality intertwined. Inspired by his love for the great outdoors and the beauty of nature, he designed vibrant landscapes filled with rolling hills, mystical forests, and a bustling village known as Inkwell Village. Each location within this animated universe was meticulously crafted to evoke a sense of wonder and adventure, inviting viewers to embark on a journey alongside The Hog.

The show's backstory was carefully woven with the inclusion of The Hog's sister, the Hogette. Davids believed in the power of familial bonds and wanted to emphasize the importance of friendship and support. The Hogette, with her spirited personality and unwavering loyalty, became the perfect companion to The Hog, adding depth to the show's narrative and establishing a heartwarming dynamic between the siblings.

To complement the whimsical world of "The Hog," Davids enlisted a team of talented artists, writers, and animators who shared his passion for bringing this imaginative vision to life. Their collaborative efforts fused together the distinctive elements of Mexican folklore, personal experiences, and creative storytelling, resulting in a rich tapestry of colorful characters, enchanting landscapes, and captivating adventures.
